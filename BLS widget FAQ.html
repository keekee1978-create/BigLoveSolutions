Top 5 Common Health Insurance Questions

1. Premium vs. Deductible vs. Out‑of‑pocket maximum? Premium = monthly cost. Deductible = you pay before insurance kicks in. Out‑of‑pocket max = the most you pay per year (then 100% covered). Example: $3,000 deductible / $6,000 max → after $6K all care is free.
2. Are preventive services really free? Yes, under the ACA: annual physical, vaccines, blood pressure screening, mammograms, well‑woman visits, contraception, well‑child visits. Caution: If a problem is treated (e.g., mole removal), that part may not be free.
3. HMO vs. PPO vs. EPO – what’s the difference? HMO: Lowest cost, need PCP referral, no out‑of‑network. PPO: Flexible, no referral, partial out‑of‑network coverage (higher premium). EPO: No out‑of‑network, but usually no referral needed.
4. How do I know if my medication is covered? Check your plan’s “formulary” (drug list) online. Log into your insurance portal or call the pharmacy benefit manager (PBM). Ask your pharmacist to run a test claim. Ask your doctor for a cheaper alternative if needed.
5. What’s a qualifying life event for Special Enrollment? Outside Open Enrollment, you can enroll within 60 days after: losing coverage (job, turning 26), marriage, birth/adoption, moving to a new ZIP/county (with prior coverage). Not qualifying: wanting better coverage or voluntary quit.
⚠️ Always verify details with your plan or HR
